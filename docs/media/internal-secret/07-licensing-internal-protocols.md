## 1. LICENSING STRATEGY OVERVIEW

### 1.1 Core Philosophy

**Tiered Control Model:**
- Core IP: Never licensed (retain 100%)
- Derivative Works: Selective licensing with approval rights
- Merchandise: Liberal licensing with quality controls
- Technology: Licensed only through partnerships

### 1.2 Priority Matrix

**Licensing Priority Order:**

| Category | Priority | Rationale |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Film/TV | 1 | Brand building, mass reach |
| Premium Streaming | 2 | Controlled narrative, subscriber base |
| Board Games | 3 | Direct monetization, fan engagement |
| Mobile Games | 4 | User acquisition, ongoing revenue |
| Apparel | 5 | Brand visibility, margins |
| Collectibles | 6 | Collector market, scarcity value |
| Food/Beverage | 7 | Mass market, limited brand impact |

### 1.3 No-License List

**Categories we never license:**
- Adult content (any form)
- Gambling (regulated)
- Weapons (real)
- Political organizations
- Tobacco/vaping
- Cryptocurrency (without specific approval)
- NFTs (until market stability)

## 2. LICENSING DEAL STRUCTURES

### 2.1 Standard Terms Framework

**Minimum Acceptable Terms by Category:**

#### Film/TV:
```
Guarantee: $250,000 minimum
Royalty: 5-7% of gross revenue
Approval: Full creative approval
Term: 5 years exclusive
Territory: Limited by region
Reversion: Rights revert if not produced in 3 years
```

#### Streaming Series:
```
Guarantee: $100,000 minimum
Royalty: 3-5% of licensing revenue OR
         Per-episode payment ($25,000-$100,000)
Approval: Script and casting approval
Term: Per-season basis
```

#### Board Games:
```
Guarantee: $50,000 minimum
Royalty: 10-12% of net sales
Approval: Final art and rules
Term: 3 years with renewal option
Print run minimums: 10,000 units Year 1
```

#### Merchandise:
```
Guarantee: Variable by category
Royalty: 8-15% of wholesale
Approval: Product samples required
Term: 2 years standard
Quality: ISO standards required
```

### 2.2 Walk-Away Points

**We terminate negotiations if:**
- Royalty below minimum thresholds
- No creative approval rights
- Term exceeds 7 years without exit
- Territory too broad without adequate guarantee
- No quality control provisions
- Indemnification is one-sided

### 2.3 Negotiation Tactics

**Anchoring Strategy:**
- Always start 20% above target
- First concession: 5%
- Second concession: 3%
- Final concession: 2%
- Never concede without getting something

**Information Control:**
- Never reveal minimum acceptable terms
- Use "I need to check with partners" for time
- Create artificial urgency with competing interest
- Always have BATNA (Best Alternative)

## 3. ROYALTY STRUCTURES

### 3.1 Calculation Methods

**Gross vs. Net:**
- Prefer: Gross revenue royalties
- Accept: Net sales with defined deductions
- Never: Net profit participation only

**Defined Deductions (if net sales):**
- Manufacturing costs: Capped at 40%
- Distribution: Capped at 20%
- Marketing: Excluded unless pre-approved
- Returns: Capped at 15%

### 3.2 Escalators

**Volume Escalators:**
```
Sales 0 - $500,000:     Base royalty
Sales $500K - $2M:      Base + 2%
Sales $2M - $5M:        Base + 4%
Sales over $5M:         Base + 5%
```

**Performance Escalators:**
```
If product reaches top 10 in category: +1% royalty
If product wins industry award: +2% royalty
If sequel commissioned: Renegotiate (advantage us)
```

### 3.3 Audit Rights

**Standard Audit Provisions:**
- Annual audit right
- 10% threshold for discrepancy action
- Licensee bears audit cost if >10% discrepancy
- Full access to sub-licensee records
- Digital sales reporting mandatory

## 4. APPROVAL PROCESSES

### 4.1 Approval Levels

**Level 1: Automatic Approval**
- Standard merchandise within guidelines
- Marketing using approved assets
- Distribution in approved territories

**Level 2: Review Required (5 business days)**
- New product variations
- Marketing outside guidelines
- Limited edition items

**Level 3: Full Approval (15 business days)**
- New product categories
- Character modifications
- Narrative extensions
- Cross-promotions

**Level 4: Executive Approval**
- Major partnerships
- Multi-territory deals
- Brand extensions
- Any deal >$500,000

### 4.2 Creative Approval Specifics

**For Film/TV:**
- Showrunner selection: Consultation
- Head writer: Approval
- Main cast: Approval
- Plot changes: Approval for lore-significant
- Marketing: Approval for entity usage

**For Games:**
- Core mechanics: Approval
- Art direction: Approval
- Character portrayal: Approval
- Difficulty balance: Consultation
- DLC content: Approval

**For Merchandise:**
- Design concepts: Approval
- Color accuracy: ±5% variance allowed
- Material quality: Sample approval
- Packaging: Approval
- Price point: Consultation

### 4.3 Response Timelines

| Approval Level | Initial Response | Final Decision |
|----------------|------------------|----------------|
| Level 1 | N/A | Automatic |
| Level 2 | 2 days | 5 days |
| Level 3 | 5 days | 15 days |
| Level 4 | 10 days | 30 days |

**Silence Rule:** Failure to respond within timeline does NOT constitute approval.

## 6. CONTRACT TEMPLATES

### 6.1 Standard Clauses

**Indemnification:**
```
Licensee shall indemnify and hold harmless Licensor from 
any claims, damages, losses arising from Licensee's products 
or services, including but not limited to product liability, 
breach of representations, and infringement claims relating 
to Licensee's modifications.
```

**Quality Control:**
```
Licensee shall maintain quality standards meeting or exceeding 
industry standards and shall submit samples of all Licensed 
Products for approval prior to manufacture. Licensor reserves 
the right to inspect manufacturing facilities upon reasonable notice.
```

**Termination:**
```
Licensor may terminate upon: (a) material breach uncured for 30 days; 
(b) bankruptcy or insolvency of Licensee; (c) quality failure; 
(d) failure to meet minimum guarantees; (e) reputational damage 
to Licensed Property.
```

**Sell-Off Period:**
```
Upon termination, Licensee shall have 90 days to sell existing 
inventory at approved prices. After sell-off period, remaining 
inventory shall be destroyed with certification provided.
```

### 6.2 Territory Definitions

**Standard Territories:**
- North America: US, Canada, Mexico
- Western Europe: EU27 + UK + Norway + Switzerland
- Asia Pacific: Japan, Korea, Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, Australia, NZ
- Greater China: Mainland China (separate negotiation always)
- LATAM: Central and South America
- MENA: Middle East and North Africa
- ROW: All others

### 6.3 Exclusivity Provisions

**Exclusivity Types:**
- Full exclusive: Single licensee, all products
- Category exclusive: Single licensee, specific category
- Territory exclusive: Single licensee, specific region
- Channel exclusive: Single licensee, specific retail/digital

**Exclusivity Requirements:**
- Always include minimum commitments
- Always include performance benchmarks
- Always include termination for underperformance
- Never grant >5 year exclusive without renegotiation clause

## 7. FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT

### 7.1 Revenue Tracking

**Reporting Requirements:**
- Monthly: Unit sales, revenue, returns
- Quarterly: Full P&L, channel breakdown
- Annual: Audit-ready statements

**Deduction Verification:**
- Manufacturing costs: Invoice review
- Distribution: Contract verification
- Returns: Physical inspection rights
- Marketing: Pre-approval requirement

### 7.2 Payment Terms

**Standard Payment Schedule:**
```
Guarantee: 50% upon signing, 50% upon first product ship
Royalties: Quarterly, net 30 days
Audit adjustments: Net 15 days
```

**Late Payment:**
- 1-30 days: 1.5% interest/month
- 31-60 days: 2% interest/month + warning
- 61-90 days: Suspension of license rights
- 90+ days: Termination right

### 7.3 Minimum Guarantees

**Setting Minimums:**
```
Year 1: Based on partner's projections ÷ 2
Year 2: Year 1 actual × 1.2 OR projected ÷ 1.5
Year 3+: Previous year actual × 1.1
```

**Shortfall Provisions:**
- Shortfall remains payable
- May count against next year (up to 25%)
- Repeated shortfall = termination right

## 8. ENFORCEMENT PROTOCOLS

### 8.1 Infringement Response

**Level 1: Individual Infringer**
```
Day 1: DMCA/Takedown notice
Day 7: Follow-up notice
Day 14: Platform escalation
Day 30: Legal review for further action
```

**Level 2: Commercial Infringer**
```
Day 1: Cease and desist (attorney letter)
Day 14: Follow-up with financial demand
Day 30: Litigation assessment
Day 45: File suit OR settlement negotiation
```

**Level 3: Organized Counterfeiting**
```
Immediate: Engage anti-counterfeiting service
Week 1: Evidence collection
Week 2: Law enforcement coordination
Week 4: Civil action
Ongoing: Customs enforcement
```

### 8.2 Licensee Violations

**Minor Violations:**
- Notification
- 15 days to cure
- Documented warning
- No financial penalty (first offense)

**Major Violations:**
- Immediate notification
- 30 days to cure
- Financial penalty per contract
- Possible license suspension

**Material Breach:**
- Written notice
- 30 days to cure (if curable)
- Immediate termination right
- Full legal remedies available

### 8.3 Dispute Resolution

**Escalation Path:**
1. Account manager negotiation (5 days)
2. Director-level negotiation (10 days)
3. Executive-level negotiation (10 days)
4. Mediation (binding if agreed)
5. Arbitration (AAA, New York, English)
6. Litigation (last resort)
